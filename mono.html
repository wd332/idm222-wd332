Through playing a game surrounding the theme of monochromes, players will understand more on how color works on the human eyes and how to differentiate them well.
This was a one week project.

My Role

In this 2-people team project, I created 3 of the mini games along with the interactions for them, determined fonts and color pallete, and error-checked everything to make sure they are consistent.

Target Audience

This game is intended to teach people on the basics of colors and monochromes through a playful, interactive way.

Emily is a high school student who is getting started in her artistic journey, beginning with the fundamentals of colors.

Research

My vision for this mini game is to create a fun and interactive game that also teaches a lesson to the user. I got this idea from two coding games that I have tried called "Flexbox Froggy" and "Grid Garden", which teaches people on how those css properties work through visual interactions.

My study has taught me that monochromes can is one color that is in a range of shades going from light to dark. You can manipulate the shades through sizes and overlapps to create distance, showing dimunition.
In color theory, the way a color's shade may be perceived by the user can be through its surroundings. A light color in a dark area may look very bright compared to it being in a light area.

Design & Prototyping

Game #1:
I wanted to make the experience enjoyable for the user through an easter egg in the game. It showed three balls and that if you click on the yellow ball, it will tell you that it's actually the sun. Small, lively dialogues like this are also enjoyable to read with emoticons to create a more fun experience.

Game #2:
I wanted to show them what exactly is a monochrome by letting users interact with the shapes getting them from lightest to darkest. I added words of "lightest" and "darkest" with their associated colors to allow the users a visual sense of what is light and dark in case they didn't know.

Game #3:
I wanted to show users the power of illusions that colors can create. Keeping consistency of monochromatic colors, I chose a base color of blue, and the light and dark colors of that. Through the interaction of clicking the squares, they can see how colors affect each other and how they see things.

Results & Outcomes

I learned that through understanding the basics of how components and variables work, I can create simple yet fun interactions for users to enjoy.

My Visual Arts professor also said they would use this as an example for future students to see.
