As part of a pre-seed startup initiative during my 10-week internship at Coded By:, our team was tasked with researching, validating, and designing a digital product that addressed a significant business problem with market potential.

Our initial concept centered around an event collaboration platform for small business owners — a space where local brands could connect and co-host marketing events to expand reach and share audiences.

After several weeks of development and mentor feedback, we were advised that our approach, while well-designed, was too narrow to appeal to a broader user base or investor interest. In response, we pivoted our product direction into a comprehensive marketing campaign builder MVP, enabling users to create, manage, and track campaigns across multiple channels such as events, social media, and email.

This case study walks through that process — from research and design to adaptation and final delivery.

My Role

I was the UI/UX Designer in this 4-people project, with the others being a designer, PM, and devloper. I worked on research, wireframing, ideation, design, and interactions.

Research

Because this project was aimed at pre-seed readiness, we began researching on what kind of problems are out there, and seeing if those problems are viable enough to be tackled. Our goal was to ensure that the pain point we addressed was large enough to justify early-stage funding.

Initial Goals:

  • Research on problems to pinpoint what is causing it through articles, blogs, and utilizing the 5 Why Method
  • Validate the problems back by creating statements and finding 5 or more articles to prove it's real
  • Calculate potential earnings through potential users and solutions already in the market

The card sort exercise conducted showed that testers would like to see the reviews of the products and additional items in the navigation to enhance the site.

Initial Solution - Event Collaboration Platform

Our first concept focused on enabling small businesses to discover partners, co-create event listings with AI assistence, and promote them collaboratively.

As one of two UI/UX designers, I focused on translating our research insights into intuitive flows and interface designs.

My Contributions Include:

  • Designing the IA and storyboard of how the user would go through each process
  • Building multiple versions of wireframes and low-fidelity prototypes in Figma
  • Created the Business Profile page, Contacts page, and AI page
  • Assisting my co-designer in understanding reasons behind out webpages
  • Create and assisted in microinteractions

Design & Prototyping

1st Wireframe

Since this was a collaboration platform, there had to be a way for people to find each other. In businesses, people would give each other business cards. I thought it would be great to introduce new people to users in such a way. I also wanted to make it easy for users to view information without a lot of distractions from the side, so my initial wireframe had the cards in the middle with some embellishments on the side for visuals.

The problem with this is that there is still too much white space, so it wasn't feasible.

2nd Wireframe

In my second wireframe, I attempted a grid layout to fill in those whitespace. However the problem now is that information is overwhelming for the user to absorb and it feels very dead with how static it feels.

3rd Wireframe

In my third wireframe, I decided to shrink the card so that there's less information for the user to see, but still enough to garner interest for them to click to view more information about their potential business collaborators. There would also be a "Recommendations" at the top that would complement the business the user has.

Even when things got smaller, it still felt dead and static. Furthermore, making it smaller didn't change the fact it was still overwhelming. I decided to look at home Airnbnb did things and big pictures helped balance things out.

4th Wireframe

I tried a new approach on things and decided to give more space on the top as a breathing space, but also so there's a clear indication that this is the business page. I also added a search bar for easier navigation in case the filters doesn't have exactly what the user wants.

I expanded my small profile picture image from the small card into one that expands and takes up the whole card. I also added an arrow so that things could continue on as a carousel.

Still, I wished it was more dynamic and had the focus I originally wanted from my first wireframe.

5th Wireframe

To create that focus that I want, I played with microinteractions. The middle card would always be larger than the other cards to create a hierarchy and allow users to focus on the middle one. When users click on the arrow, it will create a fluid movement going from one card to the next, expanding and shrinking.

Results & Outcomes

Conclusion/Modified Goals:

  • Changed the layout in a way that fits more with Zara's image of being avant garde through using a parallax grid system
  • Created a way to inform the pricing of the item in the same page
  • Shrunk images to fit the screen so that it won't overwhelm the user
  • Added a reviews section for users to see quickly as they are scrolling
  • Deleted some sections in the homepage that was unnecessary and added prices for some items there
